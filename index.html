01Data AnalysisMongoDB

Music Dataset Analysis

JSON data, aggregation pipelines, and written findings — DS4300

►

Context

This project came from DS4300, Large-Scale Storage and Retrieval. I worked with a JSON-based music research dataset containing fields like stimulus and is_attention. The task was not just to run queries. It was to understand what the data actually contained, where it was inconsistent, and what it could credibly support.

What I did

I used MongoDB and mongosh to inspect the dataset structure, then built aggregation pipelines using $match, $group, $sort, and $limit. Missing fields and empty values were common. I filtered and validated those before treating any output as final. The deliverable was a written analysis explaining the logic behind each result, not just the numbers.

What it shows

Good query output requires knowing what you are filtering and why. I iterated on the pipeline logic several times before settling on findings I trusted.

02Product ConceptEntrepreneurship

BurLook

Myanmar entertainment platform — Pitch Your Success Competition, 2023

►

Context

BurLook was developed for the Pitch Your Success Competition in Yangon, Myanmar in 2023. Myanmar entertainment audiences engage actively across film, music, and influencer media, but the infrastructure for finding and discussing that content is fragmented.

What I did

I ran a citywide survey with around 50 responses to validate who the target users were and whether real demand existed. Those findings fed directly into product and positioning decisions. I built a pitch deck and website prototype using Figma and Framer, then presented with the team. We were selected as 1 of 10 teams to present out of 22 and received strong positive feedback.

What it shows

BurLook is the clearest example of taking an idea from observation to research to something presentable. The survey work grounded the concept in real user data rather than assumptions.

03Health InnovationTeam Project

Guardia

Seizure alert and independence concept — Husky Health Innovation Challenge

►

Context

Guardia was developed for Northeastern's Husky Health Innovation Challenge. Our team focused on adults living with epilepsy. Managing seizure risk and maintaining daily independence pull in opposite directions for most users.

What we built

We moved past a basic alert system. The concept included seizure monitoring alongside mental health resources, peer community features, and tools for communicating a user's condition to the people around them. My contribution was in user needs research, concept framing, and helping translate the idea into a presentation that connected clinical realities to lived experience.

What it shows

The strongest part of this project was getting the problem definition right before building anything. A reactive alert system would have been easier to pitch but would have missed what users actually needed.

04ResearchPitch

RAIZ Pitch

Donor pitch deck — NUVIEW / Manna Project International

►

Context

This pitch connected to work with NUVIEW and Manna Project International during a five-week experiential learning program. The task was to understand MPI's Raiz program in Ecuador, find the most compelling framing of the opportunity, and build materials that communicated it to an audience without prior context.

What I contributed

I helped create an 11-slide donor-facing pitch deck in Canva, then presented as part of a three-person team across two meetings with MPI executives. My role centered on research and synthesis: pulling context from multiple sources, identifying what mattered most, and turning that into slides that were organized and readable. A large part of the work was editorial, deciding what to keep and what to cut.

What it shows

Taking something complex, finding the through line, and communicating it concisely applies across most roles I am targeting. This project was a direct test of that under real constraints.
